Mercedes-Benz was officially founded in 1926 through the merger of two pioneering companies: Benz & Cie, established by Karl Benz, and Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft, founded by Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach. The brand's roots trace back to the 1886 Benz Patent-Motorwagen, widely recognized as the first automobile powered by an internal combustion engine. The name "Mercedes" was derived from the daughter of an early automobile entrepreneur, Emil Jellinek, who marketed the vehicles in the early 1900s.
